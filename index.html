About the Live Server

This server provides live FM DX reception data using a TEF6686 tuner. The goal is to monitor long-distance FM signals, particularly during the E-Skip and Tropo seasons.

Antenna Setup

The system uses a 10 Element InnovAntenna mounted at 20' AGL. The antenna can be rotated manually and will be repositioned throughout the DX season.

  • SW (Southwest): Default orientation outside E-Skip peak.
  • NE (Northeast): Targets European DX during E-Skip season.
  • S (South): Oriented for Latin American DX opportunities.

How to deduce Antenna Orientation

  • SW: 101.9 will be stronger than 99.9. 94.7 and 97.3 will be very strong.
  • NE: 99.9, 101.1, 102.9 and 106.1 are the strongest signals.
  • S: 95.1 is similar strength to 99.9 and 101.9. Locals overall not quite as strong.

S, NE 95.7 Good DX frequency all-around. Usable to the west for Es/Tropo, but WKSS Hartford pest is an annoyance.
S, NE 96.5 Dueling pests to the west, but like 95.7 is usable that way. Very good DX frequency to the south for Latin America.
All 97.1 Almost as good as 93.9, just an NYC pest. Great channel for Meteor Scatter to the SW.
All 98.9 Premium DX channel, great for SW meteors. Listen for WUSL Philadelphia which is longest distance "regular" reception.
S, SW 100.1 Good frequency, nice and clear especially further south. 99.9 adjacent local can be a challenge, especially NE.
S, SW 102.1 Very open frequency, but can be challenged by 102.3 HD sideband to the NE, and strong analog adjancet (101.9) to SW
NE, S 104.3 Amazing frequency except for a pesky translator over 60 miles away that is somehow pretty strong to the west.
All 104.5 The best DX frequency on Cape Cod. Enjoy. Just a pest to the northwest that is manageable.
S, NE 105.5 Great open frequency with few regional pests that are all lower power. But the DX is also lower power on this channel...
S, NE 107.3 Good open frequency, particularly down the coast where 107.3 WPUR is a good tropo indicator. Pest to the NW near Boston.

Location & DX Conditions

Cape Cod is renowned for its strategic position during multi-hop E-Skip events. The unique geography allows rare reception from Europe, the Caribbean, and South America.
